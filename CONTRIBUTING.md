# Contributing

## What I'll Accept

Right now, while the volume of PRs is low, I'm happy to accept most reasonable PRs. As activity grows, I'll be more selective. Small, focused fixes, bugs, reliability, performance, or maintenance will always have the best chance of being accepted.

## What I Won't Accept

Large PRs, drive-by features, opinionated rewrites, or anything that expands scope without being asked for. If you open a 1000+ line PR full of unsolicited features, it will be closed.

## Opening a PR

- Keep it small.
- Explain what changed and why.
- Don't mix unrelated fixes.
- Include before/after screenshots for UI changes, and a short video if the change involves motion or interaction.
- If I have to guess what changed, I'm much less likely to review it.

## Issues First

For non-trivial changes, open an issue first. That doesn't guarantee I'll want the PR, but it saves you time.

## Expectations

Opening a PR doesn't create an obligation. I may close it, ignore it, ask you to shrink it, or reimplement the idea myself later. If you're fine with that, proceed.
